Individuals Who Need Safeguarding

Vulnerable Adults

  • Elderly Individuals: Often frail due to age or health issues.
  • People with Disabilities: This includes physical disabilities, learning disabilities, and cognitive impairments.
  • Substance Misusers: Adults who misuse drugs or alcohol.
  • Socially Isolated Individuals: Those who live alone and lack support from family or friends.

Children and Young People

  • Infants and Toddlers: Children from birth to around 3 years old.
  • School-Age Children: Typically aged 4 to 12 years old.
  • Adolescents: Young people aged 13 to 18 years old.
  • Children with Special Needs: Those with physical, emotional, or developmental challenges.

Specific Groups at Risk

  • Homeless Individuals: Those without stable housing.
  • Migrants and Refugees: People who have relocated, often under distressing circumstances.
  • Victims of Domestic Abuse: Individuals experiencing abuse within their homes.
  • Individuals with Mental Health Issues: Those struggling with mental health conditions that may impair their ability to protect themselves.

Key Criteria for Safeguarding

  • Age: Children and elderly individuals are often more vulnerable.
  • Disability: Physical, mental, or emotional disabilities can increase vulnerability.
  • Health Conditions: Chronic illnesses or conditions that impair judgment or mobility.
  • Social Circumstances: Isolation, poverty, or lack of support networks.
